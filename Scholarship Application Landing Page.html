FAQs on SCHOLARSHIP ASSISTANCE PROGRAM

The Scholarship Assistance Program is a government initiative designed to provide financial support to students who meet the eligibility criteria. This includes financial assistance for tuition fees, books, and other educational expenses.

To qualify, you must be a student currently enrolled in an accredited institution, not receiving any other scholarship or financial aid, belong to a family with a monthly income below P15,000, and maintain a minimum GPA of 85%.

  • Financial Assistance: Coverage for tuition fees, books, and other educational expenses.
  • Academic Support: Access to tutoring and mentoring services.
  • Career Guidance: Assistance with career planning and job placement.

To apply, you need to visit your local social welfare office or apply online through the official government portal. You will be required to submit documentation such as proof of enrollment, proof of income, and other relevant documents.

Commonly required documents include:

  • Certificate of Enrollment
  • Latest School ID
  • Barangay Certificate of Indigency
  • Digital Copy of Signature
  • Digital Copy of 1x1 Picture
  • Digital Copy of Birth Certificate
  • Digital Copy of any valid ID to confirm Age and Residency
  • Latest Report Card or Transcript of Records
